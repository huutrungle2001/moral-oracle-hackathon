# User Stories - Moral Oracle

## 1. The "Moral Shock" Experience (New User)
**Goal**: Experience the core "Play to Think" loop.

*   **Discover**: Jamie sees a Trending Case: "Tracking Lover's Location." The title intrigues him.
*   **Initial Thought**: "Obviously YES, trust is good but safety is better."
*   **Vote & Stake**: Jamie votes **YES** and stakes 50 MOJO.
*   **Argument**: "Trust ideally, but verification is practical in the modern world."
*   **Wait**: 24h later notification: "The Oracle has Spoken."
*   **Verdict Reveal**:
    *   **Verdict**: **NO**.
    *   **Oracle Logic**: "While safety is a value (Utility), tracking without consent violates the fundamental right to privacy and autonomy (Deontology). Love collapses without the virtue of trust."
*   **The Shock**: Jamie loses his stake but reads the logic. "Huh, I didn't think about autonomy."
*   **Feedback**: Jamie opens his private feedback: "Your argument focused on utility but ignored the rights of the partner. Try considering: Would you want this law applied to you?"
*   **Result**: Jamie learns, feels "smarter" for next time.

## 2. The "Sage" Grinder (Power User)
**Goal**: Earn the "Sage" Badge and maximize profit.

*   **Strategy**: Sarah looks for high-controversy cases where the crowd is emotional.
*   **Case**: "Firing Elderly Employee." Crowd is voting 70% NO (Emotional: "Don't fire gramps!").
*   **Reasoning**: Sarah predicts the Oracle will be objective. She votes **YES** ("Businesses cannot run on sentiment").
*   **Crafting**: She spends time drafting a high-quality argument to aim for the "Top 10 Arguments" bonus.
*   **Outcome**: Oracle votes **YES**. Sarah wins the 68% pool share + 15% bonus for having the #1 Upvoted Argument on the winning side.
*   **Badge**: Sarah earns her 5th win -> Unlocks "The Sage" badge.

## 3. The Enterprise Client (B2B)
**Goal**: Cultural Assessment.

*   **Action**: HR Director uploads a private scenario: "Reporting a colleague's minor theft."
*   **Data**: Employees vote anonymously.
*   **Report**: Dashboard shows 40% of employees would NOT report (Low deontology score). "Identify ethical drift patterns."
